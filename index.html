At the Surface

It started with a tiny bell.

Anna was crocheting a scarf when she realized the half-finished dome in her lap looked exactly like a jellyfish bell. So she added eight wobbly tentacles. Then she gave it to her best friend.

Her friend put it in her car. Three days later, a stranger at a stoplight rolled down their window and said, "I love your jellyfish." That was it. Smack Magick was born.

— 20 ft
Drifting Down

Every jelly is a little permission slip.

Permission to hang something whimsical from your ceiling. To clip one to your backpack at age 34. To put a glittery tentacled creature on your dashboard when you're stuck in traffic and need a reason to smile.

The yarn is iridescent — it shifts color in the sun, glows under a lamp, catches the light when your cat bats it around.

— 70 ft
The Shimmer Zone

They're built to be played with.

Every Smack Magick jelly has a long hanging loop — strong enough to swing around, soft enough to nap on. The tentacles are intentionally tangly. That's the charm.

Some live in windows. Some dangle over cribs. Some are tied to bike handlebars. One is currently, as far as we know, on a fishing boat in Maine.

— 130 ft
The Philosophy

Give one. Care for it. Share the next.

The heart of Smack Magick isn't the yarn — it's what happens after. Every jelly comes with a small tag inviting you to, at some point, pass it along. Or make one. Or just tell someone they made your day.

It's not a transaction. It's a tide.

— Anna 🌸
The three currents

A philosophy in three movements.

Smack Magick is less of a shop and more of a promise. Here's how it works.

🎁

Give

Every Smack is an excuse to show up for someone. A friend having a hard week. A kid who could use a tiny friend. Yourself, maybe.

💜

Care

Hold it gently, or swing it around — both are fine. When it gets tangled (it will), gently untangle it. That's the whole exercise.

🌊

Share

When the time feels right, pass it along. Give yours to a stranger, a neighbor, a friend who needs it more. The smack always grows.

Meet the maker

Made, one stitch at a time, by Anna King.

Anna is a knitter, a collector of weird yarn, and — as of the jellyfish incident — a practicing philosopher of small whimsical gifts.

Each Smack Magick jelly is hand-knit in her studio, usually while watching ocean documentaries. She names the yarn colors as she picks them. She will never stop saying "smack" like it's a word she invented.

If you meet Anna, she'll probably give you a jelly. That's just the rule now.

— with love, Anna 🪼
